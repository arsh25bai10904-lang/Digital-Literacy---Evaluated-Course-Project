Digital Literacy: Social Media Do's and Don'ts

The 5 Do's (Best Practices):

1) DO Check Your Privacy Settings: Check the visibility of your posts and info like phone number, email, and current location.

2) DO Think Before You Post: Consider whether you'd want a potential employer or professor to see the content you're sharing.

3) DO Verify Before Sharing: Take 30 seconds to fact-check the viral headline before clicking the 'Share' button.

4) DO Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Use unique passwords for every social media site, and turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).

5) DO Give Credit to Creators: Tag the original creator of the artwork, photo, or research you're sharing.

The 5 Don'ts (Common Pitfalls):

1) DON'T Post Sensitive Personal Info: Refrain from sharing your home address, current real-time location, or photos of ID/tickets.

2) DON'T Engage with Trolls: Steer clear of unprofessional arguments in the comments section, as they can damage your reputation.

3) DON'T Overshare Your Schedule: Be careful not to post your class schedule or the time you'll be away from your dorm/house, as this might put you in physical danger.

4) DON'T Click Suspicious Links: Be wary of links, even those sent by a friend in a DM. They might have been hacked.

5) DON'T Post Content That Violates Policies: Refrain from sharing content that might be seen as harassment, bullying, or breaking the law, as this might have academic repercussions.
