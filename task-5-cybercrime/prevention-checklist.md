To protect your digital identity and financial information, you need to be careful and implement these 8 actionable steps:

1) Verify UPI "Collect" Requests: Never enter your UPI PIN to accept money from others. If you get a "Request" or "Collect" notification from a stranger, it is a scam to deduct money from your account.

2) Enable Biometric Locks for Payment Apps: Always enable fingerprint or face ID locks for apps such as GPay, PhonePe, and Paytm to prevent unauthorized transactions if your mobile is snatched.

3) Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Always enable MFA for your college email account, LinkedIn account, and GitHub account through Google Authenticator or any other authenticator app instead of SMS-based MFA.

4) Auditing Your Digital Footprint: Google yourself and audit your social media privacy settings. Delete sensitive information such as your hostel room number or personal mobile number.

5) Identify "HTTPS" and Padlocks: Never enter your credentials or card details on a website unless you see a padlock icon and "https://" in the URL.

6) Prevent Using Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log into your bank portal or make a purchase while connected to public open-source Wi-Fi at a railway station or café without a VPN.

7) Be Cautious of "Urgent" Emails: Never open emails from "Professors" or "Admin" asking you to download files or perform urgent actions. Check the sender's email ID for spelling mistakes.

8) Regularly Update Software: Always keep your laptop’s operating system and mobile apps up to date to the latest version to prevent hackers from exploiting vulnerabilities.
